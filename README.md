## Subject Area

These disclosures address unresolved technical problems in AI-based remote examination monitoring (also known as remote proctoring or online proctoring). The proctoring industry currently lacks standard architectural patterns for:

1. Per-session dynamic sensitivity configuration responsive to examination metadata and candidate-declared accommodations.
2. Pre-decision candidate-accessible explanation of automated integrity flags with structured contextualization intake.

The disclosures here describe both architectural patterns in technical detail and release them to the public domain.

---

## Why defensive publication?

Both architectural patterns described here are likely to become regulatory requirements under emerging frameworks including:

- General Data Protection Regulation Article 22 (right to meaningful information about automated decision-making)
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act Articles 13 and 14 (transparency and human oversight for high-risk AI systems)
- ISO/IEC 17024 personnel certification standard and successor revisions (AI-disclosure provisions)
- Equivalent legislation in other jurisdictions

When such requirements become binding, every proctoring vendor will need to implement these patterns. Defensive publication ensures no single vendor can patent and use these techniques to block competitors from compliance.
